1. Research Topic and Background¶

Introduction¶

The Covid-19 pandemic has had a huge impact globally, not just on health and wellness of individuals but also on the abiliy to function in our everyday lives. It has necessitated a huge shift in our behavior in order to remain safe and help prevent the further spread of the disease in our communities.

A major revelation over this period has been the forced working from home situation for most employees at companis that had previously frowned upon remote working due to the percieved loss of productivity that would come from employees not being situated in a physical office.

I set out to explore how people were working before the pandemic versus what their work situations look like now and their attitudes surrounding working from home.

Motivation¶

As a millenial, I have also been drawn to the idea of having the freedom to structure my work days as I wish and the ability to live the lifestyle I choose anywhere in the world, while still being able to complete my work to a satisfactory standard. I had also longed for a working environment such as this but also felt shackled to the corporate idea of needing to be based within an office. This was forced upon me this year when my company closed and required all staff to work from home. I know many people who have been forced into a similiar situation and found that their working conditions and happiness changed drastically. It was clear that this would be an excellent area for a research survey project as companies gloablly are now considering a shift towards remote work as it helps to cut their costs and can increaser staff efficieny and happiness. The results of this survey will be of particular interest to both companies and to employees.

Sample and Population¶

For the purpose of this study I chose took a sample of my Linkedin network and their connections, the sample was simply selected by those who took the time to fill in my survey as I requested in a Linkedin post. This sample would relate to the full population of Linkedin users who may or may not have seen a change in their working environment as a result of the forced shift to remote working as result of the Covid-19 pandemic.

Research Question¶

Objective: Understand how working from home has affected employees and their attitudes surrounding it.

In order to achieve this objective, I came up with the following 3 research questions to which my survey questions would map to:

  1. Are employees as efficient and productive while working from home as they would be in the office?
  2. Are companies able to communicate effectively with their employees?
  3. Are employees as happy working from home as they would be in the office?

Scope: A sample of at least 30 respondents from a population defined as my Linkedin network and their connections.

Domain Concepts¶

Remote working or working from home is taken to mean any employees who are completing their daily tasks and duties

2. Survey design¶

When designing this survey I tried to keep the following survey design principles in mind:

  1. Keeping the instructions as clear and concise as possible
  2. Avoiding bias in the questions wording
  3. Making the survey as simple as possible for the respondents to complete
  4. Keep it short so as not to lose respondents attention span
  5. Make the answers easy enough to analyse

I decided to use a mix of some open and some closed questions in order to gather the necessary information. I tried to limit the amount of open questions in order to reduce the complexities surrounding analysis of free-text entries. The answer choices have been mostly limited to a selection of answers. There are also some likert scale type questions that have been included which map to the main research questions designed above.

The structure of this survey is as follows:

  • Demographic questions- Key demographics included in the survey are age, country of residence, industry etc
  • Univariate style questions - these questions are used to gauge respondents opinions on the subject
  • Likert scale questions - these map directly to the research questions which the survey aims to solve

For reference I have included a copy of the survey that respondents completed as a pdf document with the submission of this notebook.

From the outset I have also tried to be aware of how biases may arise from the study. One possible area for bias is in the sample of respondents from the overall population. This arises because the population of interest for this survey is the my network on Linkedin and because the sample has not been randomly selected to complete this survey but instead were asked to do it as a favour, there is room for bias because we only consider the answers of those who have taken the time to complete the survey. This is a major problem in all surveys and thus many survey researchers use some sort of incentive in order to entice potential respondents to complete their survey.

4. Conclusion and Discussion¶

Conducting this survey has been extremely interesting to see how people in different industries and fields of work are having to adapt to the current global crisis. In my own personal life I have seen the huge benefits of being able to work remotely. As indicated by respondents, the biggest benefit to them is the increased flexibilty in their schedule that they derive from working remotely. Extra time with family has also been shown to be a big factor when respondents are faced with a choice of working remotely or based in an office.

We have also seen that for all of the three research questions as defined in the introduction, the questions that mapped to them with likert data saw a vast majority of respondents agreed with the statements. From this we can conclude that for our sample of survey respondents they equally or more efficient working remotely, able to communicate as effectively working remotely and are as happy wourking remotely as they would be if they were in an office environment.

In order to improve this study, I would ideally like to sample a much larger group from the sample population. I think that because I only recieved 33 answers to my survey there is a potential that the answers have been skewed/biased due to the specific demographics of the group of respondents. With a larger sample size we may have a demographic that is much more representative of the population and thus our resulyts will be much more accurate and we will be able to draw better conclusions from them.
